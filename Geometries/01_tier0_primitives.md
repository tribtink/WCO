# Tier‑0 primitives

Tier‑0 primitives are the **irreducible conceptual atoms** from which all higher‑level structures in the substrate are generated.

> “Let’s treat this like a generative grammar: Tier‑0 are your primitives; Tier‑1 are the first composite structures you must get if the ontology is real.”   

The Tier‑0 set:

- Reality  
- Information  
- Epistemics  
- Power  
- Agency  
- Incentives  
- Trust  
- Conflict containment  
- Transformation  
- Objective function  

## Reality

**Reality** is the underlying environment in which agents, institutions, and systems exist. It includes physical, social, economic, and informational substrates.

- **Role:** provides constraints and affordances.  
- **Invariants:** reality is shared, even when perceptions diverge.  

## Information

**Information** is the content that can be transmitted, stored, and transformed.

- **Role:** mediates coordination and miscoordination.  
- **Key aspects:** signal, noise, bandwidth, latency, integrity.  

## Epistemics

**Epistemics** concerns how agents form, update, and justify beliefs.

- **Role:** shapes the quality of decisions and predictions.  
- **Key aspects:** evidence, inference, feedback, error correction.  

## Power

**Power** is the capacity to reliably influence states of the world.

- **Role:** determines which trajectories are feasible.  
- **Key aspects:** resources, coercion, persuasion, structural leverage.  

## Agency

**Agency** is the capacity to select actions based on goals, models, and constraints.

- **Role:** turns potential into actual trajectories.  
- **Key aspects:** autonomy, bounded rationality, capabilities.  

## Incentives

**Incentives** are the structures that make some actions more attractive than others.

- **Role:** align or misalign individual and collective behavior.  
- **Key aspects:** payoffs, externalities, feedback loops.  

## Trust

**Trust** is the expectation that others will act in ways that are predictable and non‑adversarial relative to one’s interests.

- **Role:** reduces transaction costs and enables cooperation under uncertainty.  
- **Key aspects:** reliability, reputation, shared norms.  

## Conflict containment

**Conflict containment** is the set of mechanisms that prevent conflicts from escalating beyond certain bounds.

- **Role:** keeps adversarial dynamics within survivable regimes.  
- **Key aspects:** norms, institutions, enforcement, buffers.  

## Transformation

**Transformation** is the capacity of a system to change its own structure, rules, or objectives.

- **Role:** enables adaptation, reform, and regime change.  
- **Key aspects:** plasticity, path dependence, lock‑in.  

## Objective function

**Objective function** is the (possibly implicit, distributed, or contested) criterion by which outcomes are evaluated.

- **Role:** orients trajectories and trade‑offs.  
- **Key aspects:** explicit vs implicit, local vs global, aligned vs misaligned.  

These primitives are not “true” in a metaphysical sense; they are **chosen for generative adequacy**—if they can generate the observed Tier‑1 structures, the ontology is doing useful work.
