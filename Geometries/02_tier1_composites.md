# Tier‑1 composites

Tier‑1 composites are the **first‑order structures** that must emerge if the Tier‑0 ontology is real.

> “Let’s treat this like a generative grammar: Tier‑0 are your primitives; Tier‑1 are the first composite structures you must get if the ontology is real.”   

They include:

- agents and environments  
- games and interactions  
- institutions and protocols  
- markets, hierarchies, networks  
- epistemic commons and propaganda systems  
- deterrence, escalation, bargaining  
- peace, war, and hybrid regimes  

## Agents

**Agents** are bundles of:

- access to reality (sensors, interfaces)  
- information and epistemic processes  
- power and capabilities  
- incentives and objective functions  

Agents can be individuals, organizations, states, or other entities.

## Environments

**Environments** are the contexts in which agents act:

- physical, economic, informational, institutional  
- with constraints, resources, and feedback  

Agents and environments co‑evolve.

## Games and interactions

Given agents and environments, **games** arise as structured interactions with:

- strategy spaces  
- payoffs (incentives)  
- information structures (complete/incomplete, symmetric/asymmetric)  

These can be cooperative, competitive, or mixed.

## Institutions

**Institutions** are durable patterns of rules, roles, and enforcement mechanisms.

- encode conflict containment  
- shape incentives  
- stabilize expectations  

They are composites of power, incentives, trust, and transformation capacities.

## Markets, hierarchies, networks

These are **canonical coordination architectures**:

- **Markets:** decentralized price‑mediated coordination  
- **Hierarchies:** centralized command and control  
- **Networks:** distributed, relational coordination  

Each can be represented as a particular topology on the structural axis.

## Epistemic commons and propaganda

From information and epistemics, we get:

- **epistemic commons:** shared spaces for belief formation and updating  
- **propaganda systems:** adversarial manipulation of information and epistemics  

These structures heavily influence transition dynamics and invariants.

## Peace, war, and hybrid regimes

Peace and war are not separate ontologies; they are **regimes** in the same geometry:

- peace: high trust, strong containment, high epistemic quality  
- war: low trust, failed containment, degraded epistemics  
- hybrid: mixed or unstable combinations  

Tier‑1 composites are the “standard objects” that political science and IR talk about, but here they are **explicitly generated** from Tier‑0 primitives.
