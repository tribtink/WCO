# Example trajectories

To make the geometry concrete, we sketch example trajectories through the state–context space.

> “Trajectory 1: Stable peace → internal war (scope = state). Variables: T: cross‑faction trust (0–1), C: containment strength (0–1), E: epistemic quality (0–1), G: grievance/pressure (0–1), P: power asymmetry (0–1), κ: context (−1 = hostile, +1 = cooperative).”   

## Trajectory 1: Stable peace → internal war (state level)

### Initial state

- **Scope:** state  
- **Regime:** stable peace  
- **Variables (schematic):**  
  - \(T \approx 0.8\) (high cross‑faction trust)  
  - \(C \approx 0.9\) (strong containment institutions)  
  - \(E \approx 0.8\) (high epistemic quality)  
  - \(G \approx 0.2\) (low grievance)  
  - \(P \approx 0.4\) (moderate power asymmetry)  
  - \(\kappa \approx +0.6\) (cooperative context)  

### Perturbations

- economic shocks increase **G**  
- targeted disinformation degrades **E**  
- polarizing rhetoric reduces **T**  
- institutional erosion weakens **C**  

### Transition

As **E** falls and **G** rises:

- trust \(T\) declines  
- containment \(C\) becomes brittle  
- actors update their expectations toward adversarial models  
- small conflicts are less effectively contained  

Once \(T\) and \(C\) cross critical thresholds, the system enters a **runaway escalation regime**, moving into the internal war region.

### End state

- **Regime:** internal war  
- **Variables (schematic):**  
  - \(T \approx 0.1\)  
  - \(C \approx 0.2\)  
  - \(E \approx 0.3\)  
  - \(G \approx 0.9\)  
  - \(P \approx 0.7\)  
  - \(\kappa \approx -0.5\)  

The trajectory is not a jump between categories; it is a **path** through the geometry.

## Trajectory 2: Limited interstate war → cold peace

We can similarly model:

- two states with low but nonzero trust  
- containment mechanisms that fail partially (limited war)  
- external pressure and internal fatigue pushing toward de‑escalation  
- negotiated settlements that rebuild **C** and partially restore **T**  

The end state is a **cold peace**:

- higher **C** than in war  
- moderate **T** (fragile, conditional)  
- improved **E** but with persistent propaganda residues  
- reduced **G**, but with unresolved historical narratives  

## Use of trajectories

These trajectories are:

- **illustrative**, not predictive  
- **compatible** with many specific models (game‑theoretic, agent‑based, etc.)  
- **anchored** in the same variables and invariants defined elsewhere  

They show how the geometry can be used to reason about **paths**, not just **points**.
