2. Institutions and protocols
Tier‑0 used: Power + Incentives + Conflict containment + Trust + Transformation

Institutions, protocols, enforcement, legitimacy as structured uses of power + incentives + containment.

7. War, peace, and regimes
Now plug Tier‑0 directly into your peace/war framing.

Tier‑0 used: Power + Incentives + Trust + Conflict containment + Transformation + Objective function + Information + Epistemics

Peace regime:  
A configuration where conflict is contained primarily by institutions, norms, and non‑violent transformations; trust and epistemic commons are sufficiently intact.

Peace
≈
(
High containment
,
High trust
,
Low direct coercive transformation
)
War regime:  
A configuration where conflict containment has partially failed, and power is applied through violent transformations; trust collapses across key boundaries.

War
≈
(
Low containment
,
Low trust
,
High direct coercive transformation
)
Hybrid regime:  
Mixed use of violent and non‑violent tools under partial containment (cold war, proxy war, sanctions + limited strikes).

Tier‑1 concepts:  
Peace regimes, war regimes, hybrid regimes as specific regions in your geometry, built from Tier‑0 primitives.

8. Why this matters for you
Tier‑0 is your minimal ontology.
Tier‑1 is your first derived basis—the standard objects everyone already talks about (markets, states, war, peace, institutions, propaganda, deterrence), but now:

each has an explicit decomposition into Tier‑0 primitives

you can show how changing a primitive (e.g., trust, epistemics) changes the Tier‑1 object

you can model transitions: peace‑regime → war‑regime as a trajectory in Tier‑0 space
