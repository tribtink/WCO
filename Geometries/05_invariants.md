# Invariants

In this geometry, **invariants** are properties that remain structurally true across very different regimes:

> “In this substrate, invariants are the things that stay structurally true across: peace ↔ war, cooperation ↔ adversariality, individual ↔ civilization.”   

They are not constants in the sense of never‑changing values; they are **conserved structural relationships**.

## Ontological invariants

- There are always **agents** embedded in **environments**.  
- There are always **relations** (edges) between entities.  
- There is always some **flow of information**.  

## Structural invariants

- Coordination always occurs on some **topology** (graph, network, hierarchy, etc.).  
- Power and capabilities are always **distributed**, never perfectly uniform.  
- There are always **bottlenecks** and **choke points** in the structure.  

## Epistemic invariants

- Agents always operate under **bounded epistemics**.  
- There is always some degree of **misalignment** between reality and beliefs.  
- Feedback loops between action and observation are never perfect.  

## Power and agency invariants

- Some agents have more **leverage** than others.  
- Power can be exercised through **coercion**, **incentives**, or **structure**.  
- Agency is always **constrained** by both reality and other agents.  

## Incentive invariants

- Behavior is always shaped by **incentive gradients**.  
- Misaligned incentives can produce **collective pathologies** even when individual agents are “reasonable.”  

## Trust and legitimacy invariants

- Trust is always **costly to build** and **cheap to destroy**.  
- Legitimacy depends on both **outcomes** and **perceptions**.  

## Conflict invariants

- Conflict is **structurally possible** in any regime.  
- Containment mechanisms can **shift** or **transform** conflict, but not eliminate the possibility.  

## System‑objective invariants

- Every system has some **effective objective function**, even if implicit or contested.  
- Misalignment between local and global objectives is a persistent source of tension.  

These invariants provide **anchors** in the geometry: even as systems move between peace and war, cooperation and adversariality, individual and civilization scales, certain structural truths remain.
